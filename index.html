Education

2012–2014
Masters of Nanoscience
Københavns Universitet
Principal Subjects:nanofabrication, thermodynamics, statistics, X-ray
Thesis Title: III-V Semiconductors Nanowires growth and characterization
Supervisors: Peter Krogstrup, Jesper Nygård ; Niels Bohr Institute
In this work we explored the fabrication of substrates for nanowire growth and the dynamics of their growth under X-ray with the goal of understating the fundamentals of the mechanism of the epitaxial growth of nanowires. In-situ X-ray studies are performed with a unique set-up: a molecular beam epitaxy (MBE) system mounted on a surface diffractometer at SPring8 synchrotron, Japan.
2008–2012
Bacheleor of Material Science
Ca’ Foscari University, Venice
Principal Subjects:physics of matter, chemistry, physical chemistry, mathematics
Thesis Title: A Low-Cost Approach to Increase the Efficiency of Commercial Solar Cells: Sunlight Radiation Down shifting in Cover Glass Doped with Copper by Ion Exchange
Supervisors: Elti Cattaruzza, Stefano Polizzi, Alberto Quaranta
n this work we explored the possibility to dope with transition elements (TE) - such as copper and silver - a silicate glass sheet usually used for protecting the commercial photovoltaic solar panels. The aim is to take advantage of the luminescence properties of these ion exchanged glasses that result in emission in the visible range as a consequence of the absorption in the UV region.
